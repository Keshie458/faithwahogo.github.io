Charles Bukowski

Charles Bukowski, born on August 16, 1920, was a renowned American poet, novelist, and short story writer.
Bukowski's best-known works include "Post Office," "Factotum," and "Ham on Rye,"
which draw heavily from his own experiences and reflect his disdain for societal norms

Charles Bukowski passed away on March 9, 1994, but his legacy endures through his impactful and often controversial body of work.
